01 My Process

I began my designs by exploring point, line and colour through abstract drawings of traditional Egyptian heritage points. I used Procreate (iPad) for these illustrations, using a variety of brushes to emphasise texture. My colour scheme emulated the original colours of Ancient Egyptian sculpture and paintings that were used: natural pigments, minerals, precious oils (frankincense and myrrh) and gems (Lapis Lazuli and Turquoise).

02 The Design

Following my design process, I exported my illustrations as PNGs into Illustrator, to further manipulate the designs and convert to meshes/SVGs for Blender3D. This step-by-step technique gave me ample freedom and creative expression to explore my vibrant colour scheme and abstracted style.

The Coin Design: How could I add value to a futuristic coin, in a world where many valuable natural resources have been submerged underwater?

My coin designs explore themes of flora and landscape. The ‘body’ of the coin is made from a transparent, bio-resin casing shaped, minted and reeded as a coin. Inside the resin casing I am trapping a specimen of ‘value’ inside. For example, the 10-cred coin contains a sample of ombre/pink sand preserved from the unique landscapes of the ’Lost City’. This specimen provide significance, worth and value to the currency while paying homage to its beauty. The coin can be valued as an historical, collectable artefact in centuries to come. I have transferred this design from 2D into a 3D render where I can explore its unique materials, luminosity and texture further.
